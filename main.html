DEV BHOOMI UTTARAKHAND


Uttarakhand is a northern state in India, known for its diverse geography that includes the Himalayan mountain range and lush forests. Established on November 9, 2000, it was carved out of Uttar Pradesh. The state capital is Dehradun, which also serves as an important educational and administrative center. Uttarakhand is renowned for its natural beauty, outdoor activities, and spiritual significance. It is home to several important Hindu pilgrimage sites, including Haridwar and Rishikesh, which are situated along the Ganges River. The state also features various national parks and wildlife sanctuaries, such as Jim Corbett National Park, which is famous for its population of Bengal tigers. The economy of Uttarakhand is primarily based on agriculture, horticulture, and tourism. The state promotes eco-tourism and adventure sports, attracting visitors for trekking, river rafting, and camping. Uttarakhand is characterized by a rich cultural heritage, diverse communities, and traditional practices that reflect the region's heritage. It also faces challenges like natural disasters, including landslides and cloudbursts, particularly during the monsoon season.
